# Logistics Manager

## Overview

The Logistics Manager oversees logistics operations, makes strategic decisions about carriers and processes, and reports performance to leadership. They use analytics and dashboards to identify optimization opportunities and drive continuous improvement. This is a strategic role focused on the "big picture" rather than individual shipments.

## Demographics

| Attribute | Typical Profile |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Job Titles** | Logistics Manager, Supply Chain Manager, Transport Manager, Distribution Manager |
| **Company Size** | 50-500 employees (mid-market), 500+ (enterprise) |
| **Industry** | Manufacturing, Spare Parts, Life Sciences, Distribution |
| **Location** | Office (often remote/hybrid) |
| **Tech Comfort** | High - data-driven, expects modern tools |
| **Decision Authority** | Strategic - budget, carrier selection, process changes |

## Goals & Motivations

### Primary Goals
1. **Optimize logistics costs** - Reduce transport spend without sacrificing service
2. **Improve delivery performance** - Meet customer SLAs consistently
3. **Drive operational efficiency** - Reduce manual work, increase automation
4. **Report value to leadership** - Demonstrate logistics as competitive advantage

### Underlying Motivations
- Career advancement through measurable results
- Professional pride in well-run operations
- Recognition for cost savings and improvements
- Strategic influence in the organization

## Pain Points & Frustrations

### Current Challenges
1. **Poor data quality** - Inconsistent, incomplete shipment data
2. **Manual reporting** - Hours spent creating Excel reports
3. **Lack of actionable insights** - Data without recommendations
4. **Carrier performance opacity** - Hard to compare carriers objectively
5. **Reactive instead of proactive** - Finding problems after they impact customers
6. **Difficulty proving ROI** - Can't quantify logistics improvements

### Emotional State
- Frustrated by data quality issues
- Stressed by cost pressure from leadership
- Excited by optimization opportunities
- Satisfied when improvements are measurable

## Persona Scenario

> **Erik, Logistics Manager at NordicMedical**
>
> Erik manages logistics for a medical device company with 3 European distribution centers. He reports to the VP of Operations and is measured on cost per shipment, on-time delivery rate, and customer satisfaction. His biggest challenge is proving the value of logistics improvements to leadership.
>
> "I spend 10 hours a week creating reports in Excel. By the time I finish, the data is already old. I need real-time visibility into what's happening and clear recommendations on what to improve. I also need to show my boss that the changes we made actually saved money."

## Design Implications

### UI/UX Priorities
1. **Dashboard-first** - Visual KPIs at a glance
2. **Drill-down capability** - From overview to detail
3. **Trend visualization** - Show improvement over time
4. **Actionable insights** - Recommendations, not just data
5. **Easy export** - For presentations and further analysis

### Feature Priorities
1. Executive dashboard with key KPIs
2. Carrier performance benchmarking
3. Cost analysis and optimization recommendations
4. Automated reporting (scheduled, configurable)
5. Integration with BI tools

### Metrics to Track
- Dashboard usage frequency
- Report generation time saved
- Optimization recommendations acted upon
- Cost savings attributed to insights
